CarToonz

Mobility Experience Entertainment & Education

CarToonz is an interactive in-car experience designed to transform car windows into playful digital canvases where kids can draw, create stories, and explore their imagination. Developed as part of a university curriculum in collaboration with Mercedes, the project reimagines family travel as a space for creativity and connection. By turning passive rides into shared adventures, CarToonz helps children and parents bond through engaging, interactive fun.

Problem space

As part of a university collaboration with Mercedes, we were tasked to create a speculative 10–20-year in-car experience exploring hyperconnectivity, where the interior becomes the heart of interaction.

As a team of three, we brought our personal passions into the project and shared a desire to create something meaningful for children. We imagined a self-driving Mercedes interior that gives families the freedom to learn, create, and enjoy time together, guiding our brainstorming around playful learning, creativity, and cooperation. This led us to our favorite concept, "Tell the tale together," where families co-create stories with the help of an AI companion and immersive in-car visuals.

Research

To understand how children and families experience car travel today, we conducted interviews with six participants—three parents of current school-aged children (ages 3–14) and three grandparents who care for grandchildren on weekends.

As findings of research we discovered that:

Typical car scenarios

  • Daily school and kindergarten drop-offs
  • Long trips to grandparents or for errands
  • Kids start off energetic but calm down later
  • Managing different age groups' needs

In-car activities

  • Drawing, talking, and playing with toys
  • Listening to music or audiobooks
  • Playing guessing games or word games
  • Looking out the window and spotting objects
  • Using tablets sparingly for educational content

Common pain points

  • Kids misbehaving and causing distractions
  • Difficulty in keeping kids entertained
  • Noise levels and sibling conflicts
  • Safety concerns during long trips

Age-specific needs

  • Younger kids need frequent activity changes
  • Older kids may require more engaging activities
  • Different language and communication needs
  • Managing sleep schedules to avoid crankiness

Developed concept

Persona

To ground our design decisions, we created personas based on real interviews with parents and children, capturing both the playful needs of young passengers and the responsibilities of the adults who travel with them.This helped us see the car as a shared ecosystem where every feature must support both children's creativity and parents' calmness.

Design opportunities

Inspired by the question "How might we provide engaging AND safe activities that don't rely on screens?", we began exploring functions that could meaningfully support both of our personas.

User flow and wireframes

To translate our concept into a tangible experience, we mapped out the full user flow for both the child and parent interfaces.

Usability testing

To validate our interaction decisions, we tested the placement and accessibility of key functions with children aged 5–7 in a real-life setup. This helped us confirm whether the interface was intuitive and reachable.
